# Zone 2 + Fasting: The Simple Protocol That Could Add Years to Your Life

*And why the heart rate number on your watch is probably wrong*

---

I want to tell you about two things that, when combined, have more scientific backing for longevity than almost anything else in the fitness world right now. Neither of them requires a gym membership, a complicated supplement stack, or suffering through brutal high-intensity workouts.

They are: **Zone 2 cardio** and **intermittent fasting**.

Done together, they trigger a cascade of metabolic and cellular adaptations that researchers increasingly link to longer healthspan — the years you live in good health, not just the years you're alive.

But here's the catch most people miss: you're probably training in the wrong zone without even knowing it.

---

## What Zone 2 Actually Is

Zone 2 is the aerobic intensity where your body burns fat as its primary fuel — comfortably, efficiently, sustainably. It's the pace where you can hold a conversation but wouldn't want to sing. Scientifically, it corresponds to the upper boundary of your first lactate threshold, roughly 60–70% of your maximum heart rate reserve.

At this intensity, something remarkable happens inside your cells. Mitochondria — the energy factories — get stressed just enough to trigger growth and repair without accumulating the oxidative damage that comes with harder efforts. Dr. Iñigo San Millán, who has worked with Tour de France cyclists and is one of the leading researchers in this space, has described Zone 2 as the single most important training zone for metabolic health and longevity.

The research backs this up. Regular Zone 2 work improves mitochondrial density and function, increases fat oxidation capacity, lowers resting heart rate, improves insulin sensitivity, reduces cardiovascular disease risk, and activates autophagy — the cellular self-cleaning process strongly linked to longevity.

The recommended dose for meaningful adaptation is 3–4 sessions per week, 45–60 minutes each. That's a brisk walk, a light jog, a relaxed cycle, or a steady swim — nothing heroic.

---

## Why Most People Train in the Wrong Zone

Here's the problem. Most fitness apps and watches calculate your Zone 2 using the formula `220 − age × 60–70%`. It's simple. It's everywhere. And for a large portion of people, it's meaningfully wrong.

This formula uses your age to estimate maximum heart rate and then takes a straight percentage of that. It treats a sedentary 40-year-old and a lifelong athlete of the same age as identical — which is absurd. A trained person might have a resting heart rate of 45 bpm. A deconditioned person might be at 80 bpm. Their cardiovascular systems are completely different, but the formula gives them the same Zone 2 target.

**The Karvonen method fixes this.** Developed by Finnish physiologist Martti Karvonen in 1957, it incorporates your resting heart rate into the calculation:

> **Target HR = ((Max HR − Resting HR) × intensity%) + Resting HR**

The key insight is that your **Heart Rate Reserve (HRR)** — the usable range between your resting and maximum heart rate — is what actually matters, not your raw maximum. Your resting HR is the floor your heart never drops below. Ignoring it distorts the entire picture.

For a 40-year-old with a resting HR of 48 bpm, the simple 70% target is 126 bpm. The Karvonen 70% target is 142 bpm. That's a 16 bpm gap — the difference between a gentle walk and genuinely aerobic work. Training at 126 bpm for that person is essentially a recovery session, not Zone 2.

For someone with a resting HR of 78 bpm, the math goes the other way — they'd be overtrained on the simple formula.

## Fasting: The Metabolic Partner

Intermittent fasting — specifically time-restricted eating windows of 16:8 or 18:6 — triggers many of the same cellular pathways as Zone 2 training, but through a different mechanism.

When you fast, insulin drops, glycogen depletes, and your body is forced to mobilise stored fat. This metabolic switch — from glucose to fat as primary fuel — mirrors exactly what Zone 2 training trains your body to do more efficiently.

Fasting also triggers **autophagy** (cellular cleanup), reduces chronic low-grade inflammation, improves insulin sensitivity, and activates AMPK — the enzyme that acts as a master regulator of cellular energy and is a key target in longevity research.

Put simply: fasting teaches your body to access fat fuel. Zone 2 trains your aerobic system to burn that fat efficiently. They are complementary protocols that reinforce each other.

---

## The Combination Protocol

Here's what the research-informed combination looks like in practice:

**Fasting window:** Finish eating by 8pm. Skip breakfast. Break your fast around noon. That's your 16:8 window — 16 hours fasted, 8-hour eating window.

**Zone 2 timing:** Train in the fasted state, ideally in the late morning. At the 12–16 hour fasted mark, circulating fatty acids are elevated, growth hormone is up, and insulin is low. This is the metabolic sweet spot for fat oxidation during Zone 2 work.

**Frequency:** 3–4 Zone 2 sessions per week, 45–60 minutes each. You don't need to do all of them fasted — 2 of 4 is plenty.

**What you'll notice over 8–12 weeks:** resting heart rate drops, recovery between efforts improves, energy levels through the day stabilise (no afternoon crashes), and body composition shifts toward less visceral fat even without caloric restriction.

## The Longevity Angle

The research on why this combination matters for lifespan specifically comes down to three mechanisms:

**Mitochondrial health.** Zone 2 training is the most potent non-pharmacological stimulus for mitochondrial biogenesis. Declining mitochondrial function is one of the hallmarks of cellular aging. Improving it is directly correlated with healthspan.

**Insulin sensitivity.** Poor insulin sensitivity underlies most of the chronic diseases associated with shorter lifespan — type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, certain cancers, Alzheimer's (increasingly described as type 3 diabetes). Both Zone 2 and fasting dramatically improve it.

**Autophagy.** Both fasting and Zone 2 exercise independently trigger autophagy — the process by which cells recycle damaged components. This is so significant for longevity that the 2016 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine was awarded specifically for research into it.

You're not doing extreme biohacking. You're walking briskly for an hour, not eating breakfast, and doing this consistently over months and years. The simplicity is the point.
